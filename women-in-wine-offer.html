Women in Wine

Spring 2026

We are proud to celebrate the exceptional women winemakers in The Source Imports portfolio. These pioneering vigneronnes represent the finest expressions of terroir from across Europe, combining ancestral knowledge with innovative spirit, organic and biodynamic farming practices, and an unwavering commitment to authentic, handcrafted wines.

This collection features four remarkable producers: Serena and Giovanna Bagnasco of Brandini in Piedmont's Langhe, Carole Kohler of Jardins de Fleury in the Loire Valley, Aline Domingues of Menina d'Uva in Portugal's remote Trás-os-Montes, and Katharina Wechsler in Germany's Rheinhessen. Each brings a unique vision and voice to their respective regions.

Katharina Wechsler, Rheinhessen

KATHARINA WECHSLER, RHEINHESSEN

Katharina Wechsler was born in Worms and raised in Westhofen on her family's Rheinhessen bulk wine estate. After studying social science (including a year in Paris and internship in Rio de Janeiro) and working as a Junior Editor at a Berlin TV station, her mother called in 2009 to say the family farm would close without an heir. Torn between Berlin and her roots, Katharina returned home. She officially took over in 2017, rebranding the estate in her name and transitioning from bulk production to quality, terroir-oriented winemaking with hand-picked grapes from some of Germany's most famous dry Riesling sites including Morstein and Kirchspiel.

Carole Kohler, Jardins de Fleury, Loire Valley

CAROLE KOHLER, JARDINS DE FLEURY

Carole Kohler is a former chemist who left a 15-year career in the agri-food industry to "reconnect with the living and give meaning to life." She returned to school to obtain a diploma in Viticulture at Montreuil Bellay and, in 2016, revived the historic 15th-century Fleury estate on her family's lands in Thouars, replanting a small domain with local grape varieties. Based with her husband Brice Kohler in the hamlet of Fleury overlooking Thouars, Carole farms 3 hectares of vines across two distinct terroirs: 570-million-year-old Precambrian quartz-rich schist and micaschist on the south side, and Jurassic limestone on the north. Her organic and biodynamic approach yields wines that channel the voice of the land.

Aline Domingues, Menina d'Uva, Portugal

ALINE DOMINGUES, MENINA D'UVA

Aline Domingues, the youngest of four children born to Portuguese parents who immigrated to Paris in the 1960s-70s, earned three Master's degrees in Molecular Biology, Fermentation Science, and QHSE before leaving Paris in 2017 for Uva on the remote Planalto Mirandês to make experimental wine with her grandfather Americo's grapes. Her first vintage in 2017 produced just 100 liters in a building without power or electricity. "Menina d'Uva" means "Girl of the Grape," and Aline has since revitalized old vineyard plots aged 30-65 years on the Planalto Mirandês plateau at 550 meters, working exclusively with indigenous local grape varieties farmed organically on schist and gneiss bedrock.

Serena & Giovanna Bagnasco, Brandini, Piedmont

SERENA & GIOVANNA BAGNASCO, AGRICOLA BRANDINI

Agricola Brandini is run by two idealistic Bagnasco sisters who took over the family operation in 2018. Serena (born 1992) started working in the cellar and vineyards at age 18 while studying agriculture, food and wine at the University of Gastronomic Sciences in Bra. Giovanna (born 1994) joined in 2013 at age 18 while studying English, Spanish and French Literature at the University of Turin. Together they manage 25 estate hectares in high-altitude zones of La Morra, Roero, and Alta Langa at 300-450m altitude on calcareous sandstones. Certified organic since 2011, they've also introduced biodynamics and permaculture, focusing on aroma and freshness—seeking "a recipe for the Nebbiolo of the future."
